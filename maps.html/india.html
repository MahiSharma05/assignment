Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh is a state in the southern coastal region of India. It is the seventh-largest state and the tenth-most populous in the country.

Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh is home to many monuments and archeological sites, each one has a rich history. Places of worship and pilgrimages like Parasuram Kund, the 400 years old Tawang Monastery and Gorsham Chorten draw a large number of pilgrims every year.

Assam

Assam is famous for its tea cultivation and robust Assam tea. Dibrugarh, the largest city in Assam, is known as the "Tea City of India

Bihar

Bihar is famous for its rich cultural heritage, historical significance, and contributions to spirituality and education. It's renowned for festivals like Chhath Puja, ancient sites like Nalanda, and its traditional cuisine.

Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh was formed on 1 November 2000 by partitioning 16 Chhattisgarhi-speaking southeastern districts of Madhya Pradesh. Chhattisgarh is a state in central India. It is the 10th largest state in India, with an area of 135,194 km2 (52,199 sq mi) and with a population of 25.5 million.

Goa

The state of Goa, in India, is famous for its beaches and places of worship. Tourism is its primary industry, and is generally focused on the coastal areas of Goa, with decreased tourist activity inland.

Gujarat

Gujarat is the land of rich handicrafts like Patola weaving, khadi, bandhani, block printing, embroidery, namda, rogan painting, matani pachhedi, woodcrafts, metal crafts, bamboo crafts, pithora, pottery and many more handicrafts. Gujarat celebrates all regional fairs and festivals with great fervor and spirits.

Haryana

Haryana is well known for its cattle wealth and is the home of the famous Murrah buffalo and the Haryana cow. No wonder there is an abundance of milk and milk products in Haryanvi cuisine. People make butter and ghee at home and use these liberally in their daily diet.

Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh has five rivers: the Ravi, the Beas, the Chenab, the Yamuna, and the Sutlej. The mountain ranges of the Great Himalayas present under the boundary of this state have altitudes from the lowest of 450 meters and highest peaks even up to 6700 meters.

Jharkhand

Jharkhand is known as Land of Forest. There are several Wildlife Sanctuaries and National Parks including Betla National Park, Hazaribag Wildlife Sanctuary, Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary, Gautam Budha Wildlife Sanctuary, Palkot Wildlife Sanctuary and Mahuadanr Wolf Sanctuary.

Karnataka

Karnataka is a state in the southwestern region of India. It was formed as Mysore State on 1 November 1956, with the passage of the States Reorganisation ...

Kerala

Named as one of the ten paradises of the world by National Geographic Traveler, Kerala is one of the prominent tourist destinations of India, with coconut-lined sandy beaches, backwaters, hill stations, Ayurvedic tourism and tropical greenery as its major attractions.

Madhya Pradesh

The state is highly popular for its historical monuments, modern amenities, and diverse flora and fauna. Some of them include the sculptures of Khajuraho Temple, the wildlife at Bandhavgarh National Park, the architecture at Sanchi Stupa, among others.

Maharashtra

Maharashtra is the second-most populous state in India. The state is divided into 6 divisions and 36 districts, with the state capital being Mumbai.

Manipur

Manipur is a state in northeast India, with the city of Imphal as its capital. It is bounded by the Indian states of Nagaland to the north

Meghalaya

Located in North-east India, Meghalaya, also known as the Abode of Clouds, falls in one of the richest biodiversity areas in the world. The famed monsoons, the rich traditional festivals, and the dramatic terrain are just some of the many things this state is famous for

Mizoram

Known for its scenic beauty, vibrant culture, and music. The state is home to various tribes and lush hills.

Odisha

Known for its temples, dance forms, and beaches. The Konark Sun Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Punjab

Famous for its agricultural productivity, rich history, and vibrant culture. Amritsar is home to the Golden Temple.

Rajasthan

Known for its royal heritage, forts, and desert landscapes. Jaipur, the Pink City, is a major tourist attraction.

Sikkim

Renowned for its natural beauty, biodiversity, and Buddhist culture. It offers stunning views of the Himalayas.

Tamil Nadu

Famous for its temples, classical music, and cuisine. Chennai is the state capital and a major cultural hub.

Telangana

Known for its rich history, cultural diversity, and cuisine. Hyderabad is famous for its biryani and IT sector.

Tripura

Known for its cultural diversity and beautiful landscapes. The state has several historical sites and temples.

Uttar Pradesh

Famous for its historical significance, including the Taj Mahal in Agra. It is a cultural and religious hub.

Uttarakhand

Renowned for its natural beauty, spirituality, and adventure sports. The state is home toseveral Hindu pilgrimage sites.

West Bengal

Known for its art, literature, and cultural festivals. Kolkata, the capital, is famous for its colonial architecture and vibrant culture.
